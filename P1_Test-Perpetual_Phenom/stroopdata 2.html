Background Information¶

In a Stroop task, participants are presented with a list of words, with each word displayed in a color of ink. The participants task is to say out loud the color of the ink in which the word is printed. The task has two conditions: a congruent words condition, and an incongruent words condition. In the congruent words condition, the words being displayed are color words whose names match the colors in which they are printed: for example RED, BLUE. In the incongruent words condition, the words displayed are color words whose names do not match the colors in which they are printed: for example PURPLE, ORANGE. In each case, we measure the time it takes to name the ink colors in equally-sized lists. Each participant will go through and record a time from each condition. Questions For Investigation

The current dataset that we have present to us with only a small sample group of a possibly large population (whole world of human samples as it is applicable to all humans). Luckily, we do not need data of the whole population to determine that, we have to make do with the small sample set and determine whether there is statistical significance in the two tasks.

Null Hypothesis, H0¶

Null hypothesis is that there will not be difference in reading time for Congruent and Inconguent tasks. $$ H_0 : {}_{Congruent} = {}_{Incongruent}$$

Alternative Hypothesis, Ha¶

Alternative hypothesis is there will be difference in reading time for Congruent and Inconguent tasks. $$ H_a : {}_{Congruent} < {}_{Incongruent}$$

where represents the population mean

Paired sample T-test is chosen as the statistical test. The experiment has a person tested for both of the test (group of units that has been tested twice) As the sample size is small (n<30), t-test is suitable. In addition, standard deviation of the population is also unknown.
